# Insights Summary

This document summarizes key insights obtained from the Facebook Ads Performance Dashboard.

---

## 1. Overall Campaign Performance
- Campaigns achieved strong impression volumes across both platforms.
- Click activity follows the same trend as impressions, indicating healthy engagement on ads.
- Conversions are consistent and largely dependent on age demographics.

---

## 2. CTR (Click-Through Rate) Insights
- CTR varies significantly by **age group**, with younger audiences (18–24) showing stronger performance.
- Male audiences delivered slightly higher CTR compared to female in most campaigns.

---

## 3. CPC & CPA Behavior
- CPC increases during periods of higher competition (mostly visible in peak weeks).
- CPA is lowest in age groups **25–34**, meaning conversions here cost less.
- Some campaigns have high spend but low conversions → indicates poor targeting or weak creative.

---

## 4. Platform Comparison
- **Facebook** generates higher impressions overall.
- **Instagram** has lower spend but shows competitive CTR for specific age groups.
- Conversion efficiency (CPA) is generally better on Facebook.

---

## 5. Time-Based Trends
- Impressions and clicks peak near the beginning of the month.
- Mid-month shows higher spend with relatively lower conversions — potential optimization needed.

---

## 6. Recommendations
- Allocate more budget to age groups 18–24 and 25–34 for higher efficiency.
- Pause campaigns with high CPC and low conversion activity.
- Increase testing on Instagram ads targeting young audiences.
- Improve ad creatives for demographics with low CTR but high impressions.

---

## ✔ Conclusion
The campaigns overall show good performance, but optimizing spend allocation and targeting can significantly improve CTR and CPA metrics across platforms.
